they see the athlete sitting stoically in the shadows of her past glory;remembering not her purpose or direction
but the snap of her backbone—from years ago—
"hey, how are you? do you think you'll be back soon?""i'm not sure anymore"—she tells herself:
can't shejust persist, somehow,
claw her way back? forge her own path? not give up?defy the grayscale scans; ignore
the painthat which eats away at the spine and seeps through the legs.
how could it be thatshe has to come to terms with a fate this
bad though?disastrous injury has brought…

they say, you should push yourself furiously to get back on your feet;she says, accepting that there are things in life I cannot change requires courage —
actively fighting to overcome insurmountable odds isa strength indeed. and so is building perseverance, which does not need
rising abovevisible suffering,
pain or adversity—just sustained discipline, even in smooth-sailing times.
this is how you preserve self-esteemand in Islam they call this sabr. though i am not a religious disciple, i am religiously disciplined
and prove strength to othersby conscientiously cultivating mental and physical fortitude
—not through indifference;through hours of glute bridges, years of behavioral restrictions and a lifetime of strict routine.
where does being stoic get you?it gets me to
triumph and fighting spiritwithin: I am nurturing an inner faith through which peace grows, pain is controlled and fears
subside, unfamiliar narratives thatinspire patience — new direction must
diverge from canonized motivational stories,guide her own personal life, free from regret and lamenting,
and more importantly, from social expectations. they think she does not striveto redefine her mind and self…
to break free from her body's boundaries…to reclaim her agency…the conclusion is
just not right. she is lost—she was lost—but has now found herself. she is no longer unsure of her worth—
an apparition wandering through the skeleton of her former self—choosing to invest in a constructive project of self-improvement.
her victory is not found through emotional rebellion.my victory is found in stoic resilience.
