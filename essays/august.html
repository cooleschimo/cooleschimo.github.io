august

august was a little gypsy man who came in his little caravan. he did a little twirl, sang a little song, and invited me to come along. i traipsed up a hill with him and had a good pant, but a good laugh too. when i got tired, he gave me flowers — birds of paradise, hyacinths, sunflowers, hollyhocks and roses.

then he was gone. the gentle breeze carried away the petals of my youth; left me with rose-pricked fingers and blistered feet. only a stupid smile and tear streaks remain — damp tyre tracks depressed into my memory to tell me i once lived in a little gypsy caravan.
